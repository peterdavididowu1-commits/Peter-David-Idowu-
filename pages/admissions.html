Academic Foundations

Programmes Offered

Exemplary theological curriculums designed for effective ministry, leadership, and professional excellence.

Flagship Program

Diploma in Theology (Dip.Th.)

A rigorous 2-year ministerial program that combines systematic study of the Bible, sound hermeneutical practices, church leadership skills, and practical missions training. Here are the core courses offered under this curriculum:

Systematic Theology

A deep-dive exploration of central Christian doctrines, covering Christology, Soteriology, Pneumatology, Ecclesiology, and Bibliology under precise reformed parameters.

Biblical Hermeneutics

The art and science of biblical interpretation. Learn to accurately read, parse, and apply Scriptural texts within their historical, grammatical, and literary contexts.

Homiletics & Sermon Prep

Developing effective pulpit communication skills. Students study the design, crafting, and dynamic presentation of expository and topical sermons.

Church History

Tracing the expansion of the Christian church from the early Apostles, through the medieval era, the Protestant Reformation, down to modern Pentecostal movements.

Evangelism & Missions

Strategic methods for global church planting, cross-cultural evangelism, local community engagement, and practical application of the Great Commission.

Pastoral Care & Counseling

Equipping leaders with scriptural solutions for family support, youth direction, grief counseling, moral guidance, and mental wellness administration.

Enrollment Standards

Admission Requirements

DIMABIN maintains standard academic and spiritual criteria to ensure all admitted candidates align with our theological rigor and ethical mandates.

Academic Qualifications

Minimum of Secondary School Certificate (WAEC/NECO/GCE) or its professional equivalent with credit passes in English and relevant arts/social sciences.

Spiritual Recommendation

A letter of recommendation from an ordained pastor, elder, or spiritual facilitator attesting to your Christian character, active local church involvement, and moral conduct.

Personal Testimony & Calling

A written essay (minimum 300 words) describing your salvation experience, spiritual journey, and specific sense of calling to Christian theological training.

Identification Documents

Valid state identification card (National ID, International Passport, or Driver's License) along with two recent passport-sized photographs.

Dynamic Requirements space

In line with our primary motto, Fear of God Without a Mess, candidates who do not possess a verifiable track record of high moral standard and godly character will not be approved for admission, regardless of academic excellence.

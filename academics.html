Developmental Paths

Academic Divisions Tailored for Growth

We provide standard instructions split across three main pillars, carefully handled by qualified early childhood specialist coaches.

1. Infant Creche & Playgroup

Ages: 3 Months – 3 Years

Focuses on fine sensory motor active coordination, physical safe play environments, nursery nursery story reading, color identification, and secure emotional toddler growth care.

Interactive Play-Based

2. Early Years Montessori

Ages: 3 Years – 5 Years

Introducing baseline writing lines, phonic counting routines, social integration rules, simple scientific observation, and active memory retention drills.

Montessori Inspired

3. Primary Education Block

Ages: 5 Years – 11 Years

Rigorous training covering English Language reading, Quantitative mathematics, Computer science fundamentals, Civic education character, and basic agricultural sciences.

National Standard approved

Modern Learning Facilities & Resources

At His Grace, we recognize that education goes far beyond static chalkboards. Our school board constantly invests in state-of-the-art facilities designed to keep children excited about discovery:

  • Interactive Computing Center

    Equipped with functional workstations allowing primary school learners hands-on familiarity with digital systems and offline coding structures.

  • Active Reading Library Hub

    Our signature colorful library classroom, housing standard books, illustrated novels, scientific maps, and quiet seating cushions.

  • Creative Fine Arts Workspace

    Unlocking childrens' visual expressions via color mixing models, sensory clay modeling, and cultural drawings coaching.

Progress Monitoring & Evaluation

We believe in strict, objective assessment pathways that assist parents in tracking cognitive maturity levels easily.

Termly Continuous Assessment

Weekly homework sheets combined with mid-term projects contribute 40% of the total marking scheme, encouraging daily retention.

Comprehensive Exam System

Standard end-of-term examinations comprising safe testing formats and written diagnostic layouts provide 60% of score sheets.

Parent-Teacher Dialogs
